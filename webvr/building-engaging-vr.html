Lighting and Colours

  • Saturation in VR, feeling of leaving recroom.
  • Make items visually distinct
  • Highlight interactable elements
  • Give hints to how to play [this cat has a quest]
  • Use black and white sparingly as you cannot get brighter or darker
  • Gradients and textures are good
  • Large monotone areas can make the user get lost, as well as not being interesting
  • Overly detailed textures can be too noisy [where's wally]
  • Gradients provide a nice middle ground
  • Picking a skybox

Using motion

  • Moving objects
  • Moving things draw the eye
  • Even in a VR diarama some motion can bring the scene to life.
  • Motion in response to a user's gesture implies it is interactive
  • Moving the camera
  • Make it user triggered so they can brace them selves
  • Give warning, wind up the motion, shrink field of view whilst moving
  • Teleporting can lead to disorientation
  • Bobbing and shaking can induce nausea
